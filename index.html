5 Step Painting

  • Wait for Dry Weather

    Humidity means drips and slow drying, so avoid painting on a rainy day. If you must paint when it's humid, take your time â€” and take advantage of slow-drying paint to correct your errors before moving on to the next coat. But don't overwork or it will show when you're finished.

  • Surface preparation

    The plaster must be sufficiently dry and very well cured. Work should start here after 45 days of plastering. Any type of damp, damp, wet or damaged should be fixed. It should be well rubbed with stone or sand-stone. Plastered walls should be flat. If it is not level, it should be leveled by rubbing with stones. If there is any loose dirt or any other substance in it, it should be removed. It is better to wash it with lime water. This makes the plaster dry well. It should be rubbed with sandpaper or series paper.

  • Primer or lining

    After the surface preparation is done, primer or coating should be given. This primer is applied in one coat. This primer basically creates an adhesive bond between the plaster and the paint. Each gallon of primer can cover 45 square meters. Primer is applied by roller or brush. This primer is diluted with water before application. Its application creates an adhesive bond as well as smooths the surface, reduces absorbency and spreads the paint more evenly.

  • Puti

    Any cracks or problems are puttyed. It is also called Chit Puti. This putty should be dried for at least four days. Any excess putty should be scraped off. This putty is used to make the surface or the bottom of the paint more smooth. This putty is made by mixing 1 gallon of plastic paint with 1 liter of enamel paint and 25 kg of chalk powder.

  • Color Coat

    Color is coated 2-3 times. After the first coating it should be allowed to dry for 1 day. Then the second coating is applied. If the color is not good, for example, there are patches without being clean, or the color is light, then the third coating is given 3 days after the second coating. A maximum of 20% water is mixed with the first coating. A maximum of 15% water is mixed with the second coating. Do not paint on damp, damp or wet walls. Humidity should be between 20% and 50%. Care should be taken that direct sunlight does not come during paint application.

Painting Advice

1. Color Determination

This will be your own guide for choosing a color. For example, open your wardrobe and understand the inner choice in mind and select the color that your heart desires.

2. Matching The Furniture

To emphasize your furniture, choose a soft shade or a complementary shade. For example, if you have a yellow light, you can choose a very light shade of lemon for its background, to make the light more attractive.

3. Room Size

If you want your room to look bigger, choose lighter shades, but if you want to bring a more cozy and warm atmosphere to the room, go for a darker shade.

4. Color Matching

Use a color shade card when choosing colors for different parts of your home. There should be proper harmony from one room to another

5. Match With Light

When choosing a wall paint color, keep in mind the lighting in your room where natural light brings out the true color of the paint, incandescent light brings out warmth in shadows and fluorescent light brings out sharp colors.

6. Pricing Of Paint

Now decide which company colors to use according to your ability, such as Berger, Elite, Asian, etc. The price of each color is different.
